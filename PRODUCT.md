# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Potential clients in a stressful legal situation: facing criminal charges, a labor dispute, or a family law crisis. Buenos Aires, Spanish-speaking. They arrive scared or overwhelmed, often having already searched for answers without finding clarity. They are not legal professionals; they are people who need to decide, within seconds, whether to trust this person with something that matters.

Secondary: people researching on behalf of a family member or friend in crisis.

## Product Purpose

A conversion surface for **Fabio Mombello y Asociados**, a Buenos Aires law firm with 20 years of experience in criminal, labor, and family law. Success means a visitor — mid-crisis, on a phone, at 11pm — feels three things in order: *this person is competent*, *this person is trustworthy*, *I can reach him right now*. Then they tap the WhatsApp button.

Every design decision traces back to that moment. This is not a portfolio or a showcase. It is a decision surface.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative. Measured. Human.

Not cold institutional authority — the kind of authority that comes from someone who has seen this situation before and knows what to do. Not clinical distance — the warmth of a professional who explains rather than lectures. The voice is a person's voice, not a firm's marketing department.

Tone reference: the way a confident doctor explains a diagnosis. Serious without alarm. Clear without condescension. Reassuring because competent, not because soft.

## References

**El País (the institution, not the aesthetic lane):** What to take from it — the typographic authority of strong serif display type, high contrast black on white, zero decorative clutter, institutional breathing room. The sense that the words carry the weight, not the decoration. What NOT to take — the editorial-magazine aesthetic lane (italic drop caps, tracked eyebrows above every section, broadsheet column grids, ruled separators as decoration). The authority, not the art direction genre.

The reference is the *effect*, not the aesthetic family: a visitor should read the heading and feel they are dealing with someone serious, not someone who has a nice website.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate law firm clichés**: dark navy + gold, marble textures, scales-of-justice imagery, stock photos of suited people shaking hands. Visual grammar that signals "generic law" before the visitor reads a word.
- **Startup / SaaS aesthetic**: gradient blobs, glassmorphism, cream or warm-neutral backgrounds, ultra-rounded cards, hero metric grids. Wrong register entirely.
- **Cheap local directory**: yellow pages energy, cluttered layout, low contrast, no visual hierarchy. Destroys trust immediately.
- **Generic Argentine abogados sites**: the formulaic template — blue/white, stock courtroom photos, dense column text, no personality. The site Fabio must be distinguished from.

## Design Principles

1. **Competence through restraint.** Every element removed is a trust signal added. The site does not need to look impressive; it needs to feel reliable. Decoration that doesn't serve the conversion goal is noise that erodes credibility.

2. **Authority without distance.** Serious without being cold. The visitor is scared — they need to feel heard, not processed. Typography, copy, and spacing should communicate weight and care, not bureaucratic efficiency.

3. **One clear action per fold.** The hero is a decision moment. At every scroll depth, there is one primary CTA visible: WhatsApp, call, or book a consultation. Never two competing actions at the same visual weight.

4. **Human voice before firm voice.** Copy speaks as Fabio, not as "Estudio Mombello y Asociados." First person where appropriate. Specific over generic. "He seguido este tipo de caso durante 20 años" lands harder than "Nuestra firma cuenta con amplia experiencia."

5. **Mobile-first because crisis is mobile.** The visitor in crisis is on a phone. Typography, touch targets, and CTA placement must perform at 375px before anywhere else.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. High-stakes emotional context means legibility is not a checkbox — it is part of the trust signal. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast always. No reliance on color alone to convey meaning. Framer Motion animations must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable and screen-reader labeled in Spanish.
